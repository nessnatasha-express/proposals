Key Considerations for Marketing Cloud Intelligence with Marketo

We understand the potential move to Adobe Customer Journey Analytics (CJA) might feel concerning, especially after the investment in Marketing Cloud Intelligence and the challenges you've had so far. While CJA is a great solution, this document will highlight how it is possible to continue to utilize MCI, should you choose that option. We'll walk you through how it will connect to Marketo and the rest of your tech stack, as well as highlight the architecture, pros and cons and a plan forward.

Why Was CJA Recommended?

Our initial recommendation for CJA was based on the understanding that you were in the early stages of developing journey-based analytics. CJA seemed a logical fit to deliver cross-channel insights, automate the SFMC team's manual reporting, and provide a user-friendly, cost-effective solution.

Now, with a clearer picture of your team, technology and the time already invested in Marketing Cloud Intelligence, we understand that migrating yet again might not be an option. This document outlines the strategy for staying with MCI and creating a direct integration with Marketo, as well as how we'll support you in both the integration and optimization of your current MCI setup.

How this Works

Getting Marketo and MCI connected is achieved through a robust data pipeline. The magic of making the data connect accurately and in sync happens in the "Transform" stage, where we harmonize data from all your platforms. This allows you to analyze a "campaign" from Marketo, YouTube, and your DSP (advertising) together using shared Identifiers, Dimensions, and Measurements, resulting in a comprehensive, unified view of performance in your MCI Pages. Represented below is the ETL process that takes place before it lands in the MCI dashboards.

Extract

  • API Connector Data Streams
  • Marketo Real-time Webhooks
  • Scheduled Batches (TotalConnect)

Transform

  • Data Model Mapping
  • Harmonization Center Rules
  • Calculated Dimensions & Measures

Load

  • Datorama Data Lake
  • Data Quality Validation
  • Staging Tables

Serve

  • API Endpoints
  • Dashboard Pages & Widgets
  • Real-time Updates

Platform Comparison: MCI vs. CJA

To provide a clear picture, here’s a high-level comparison of the two platforms based on our current understanding of your needs.

Marketing Cloud Intelligence (MCI)

Pros

  • Leverages existing investment and setup.
  • Platform-agnostic; connects to hundreds of sources.
  • Highly customizable data model for bespoke KPIs.
  • Faster time-to-value by enhancing a current system.

Cons

  • Higher licensing cost.
  • Requires specialized expertise to manage data model.
  • Current data integrity issues need to be resolved.

Customer Journey Analytics (CJA)

Pros

  • Purpose-built for cross-channel journey analysis.
  • Generally lower licensing cost.
  • User-friendly interface for marketers and analysts.
  • Strong integration within the Adobe Experience Cloud.

Cons

  • Requires a new implementation (rip-and-replace).
  • Less flexible for non-Adobe data sources.
  • Longer time-to-value due to new setup.

The Advantages of Sticking with MCI

Here are some key items to consider while evaluating if it makes sense to retain your current platform or move to a new one.

Although SFMCi is now part of the Salesforce ecosystem, this wasn't always the case. The platform continues to connect with numerous data sources, which helps avoid vendor lock-in and enables you to assess your entire marketing ecosystem fairly. During the review of your onsite instance, it's encouraging to see that you have a separate instance from the standard SFMC one, providing greater independence from the current MC product used in marketing.

Since you have already invested significant time and money into MCI, it may be more beneficial to enhance your existing system and incorporate the new Marketo integration, rather than starting from scratch with CJA. This would require enhancements to the current MCI instance, but should allow us to speed up the integration of analytics since all other channels are already tied in.

This initiative is larger than just addressing and supporting analytics needs - it’s also the start of the Marketing team’s ability to grow and optimize. This is why implementing Marketo and deprecating Salesforce Marketing Cloud Engagement is so important! By collaborating on this approach, we can provide assistance in marketing engagement, campaign management and email strategies, all while facilitating prompt improvements to your existing analytics system.

Optimizing the Connection of Your Full Tech Stack

Here’s a look at how all the pieces will fit together. Marketo and Salesforce will have a direct, bidirectional sync for sales and marketing alignment. All platforms, including the synced data from Marketo and Salesforce, will then feed into the central ETL process for harmonization and analysis.

Our Phased Integration & Optimization Plan

In the event you'd like to stick with MCI, knowing it is YES indeed possible it can connect to Marketo, here are some of the steps we'd work on together throughout the project to ensure you have a far better experience this time around that will vastly differ from your last agency experience, allowing you to feel more trust in a 3rd party partner, but more importantly the data you live and work in everyday.

1

Analyze Current State

Conduct a deep-dive audit of your existing MCI Workspace, including all Data Streams, the current Data Model, and Harmonization Center rules. This will allow us to pinpoint the real issues you're seeing today within your YouTube to MCI and Google Analytics to MCI connections and work on a plan to resolve them.

▼
2

Define Marketo Schema Mapping

Work with your team to map Marketo objects (Programs, Activities) to MCI Entity Types, and define key Measurements (MQLs, Funnel Velocity) and Dimensions (Program Channel, Campaign Theme).

▼
3

Establish Governance & Gain Approval

Present a unified Data Model structure, including clear data governance protocols and KPI alignment, for stakeholder approval before technical implementation begins.

▼
4

Connect Marketo & Align Workflows

Configure the Marketo API Data Stream and work with marketing operations to ensure critical Marketo Program Tags and workflows align with the new reporting structure.

▼
5

Modify Dashboards & Reporting

Update existing MCI Pages and Widgets to incorporate Marketo data, and create new Dashboard Collections to visualize the end-to-end customer journey.

▼
6

Transition & Go-Live

After rigorous validation and final approval, formally disconnect legacy SFMC Data Streams and fully transition to Marketo as the primary marketing automation data source.
