Primary Finding:

The WordPress site appears technically self-contained, with no immediate signs of proprietary technology that would prevent Young Life from taking full ownership. However, there are significant issues with best practices and maintainability that should be addressed for a smooth transition.

1. Theme & Branding Analysis

Ownership & Licensing

The theme folder is `n2-younglife`. It's critical to review the contract to confirm who legally owns this custom theme. If ownership is not with Young Life, this could be a major contractual obstacle.

Hard-Coded Elements

Key parts of the site (navigation, styles) are hard-coded into theme files. This makes the site difficult for non-developers to manage and limits the ability to make quick brand updates using WordPress's standard controls.

CSS Customization

There is no apparent customization of CSS in the WordPress "Appearance" area, which is a good thing for the migration as it simplifies the process.

2. Content & Structure

Pages vs. Theme Templates

There is a lack of actual pages in the admin area. It also looks like the bulk of what is "pages" is based outside of the site. Is the intent to have two separate sites or merge the two? There could be some critical issues with the merging approach if not done correctly and we want to call that out as a major risk. Much of the site's content is built directly into theme templates for posts, which makes a lot of sense, but for pages it does not and is typically not a scalable or user-friendly practice.

Post Structure

The setup for blog posts, categories, and tags is standard and uses core WordPress functionality correctly. No issues are foreseen with migrating this content.

3. User & Access Management

Critical Warning

Do not delete an agency user without first re-attributing their content. Doing so could result in the permanent deletion of all pages and posts created by that user.

Transition Process

A safe transition requires creating a new Young Life admin user, re-attributing all agency-owned content to the new user, and then downgrading or deleting agency accounts.

4. Plugins, Hosting & Technical Health

Hosting Ownership

It is essential to confirm that the WP Engine account is owned by and billed to Young Life. If the agency owns the hosting, you don't truly own your site and must migrate.

SEO Implementation

A major missed opportunity. While Rank Math SEO is installed, it is not being utilized. Page titles, meta descriptions, and other on-page SEO elements are largely absent.

PHP Security Notice

The security error flagged during the initial review should be investigated and resolved by a developer, as it may indicate outdated or insecure code.
