Implementation options

Calix is at a key moment for its marketing and customer engagement technology. The decision to move from Pardot isn't just about choosing a new platform for the marketing team—it's a chance to build a forward-thinking MarTech stack that truly supports your B2B goals and empowers both the Marketing and Customer Success teams.

This section lays out two distinct strategic options, giving you a full view of the possibilities this investment opens up for your teams. Each option strikes a different balance between platform consolidation, best-in-class tools, efficiency, and long-term flexibility.


Click or tap on the 2 options below to learn more about each one of them.

This approach involves migrating the Marketing team from Pardot to a new, dedicated Business Unit (BU) within the existing Salesforce Marketing Cloud (SFMC) instance currently used by the Customer Success team. This path prioritizes unifying all marketing and customer communication on a single, powerful Salesforce platform, maximizing your existing investment and leveraging the native, seamless data connection to your CRM.

Pros

  • Platform Consolidation: Unifies both Marketing and Customer Success teams onto a single platform (SFMC), simplifying vendor management.
  • Leverages Existing Investment: Maximizes the value of your current SFMC license and leverages existing internal knowledge.
  • Native CRM Integration: Offers the tightest possible data sync with your Salesforce CRM via Marketing Cloud Connect.
  • Shared Capabilities: Allows for shared assets, templates, and data models between BUs, fostering brand consistency and operational efficiency.

Cons

  • More B2C-Centric Model: SFMC's contact-based data model is not optimized for ABM and requires significant custom data architecture for B2B.
  • Complex Integrations: Integrating account-based tools like 6sense is not native and requires a custom solution.
  • Potential for Conflict: Shared resources and platform limits require strong governance to prevent teams from impacting each other's performance.

This option represents the "Total Unification & B2B Alignment" strategy. It involves a full consolidation of all marketing and customer engagement efforts by migrating both the Marketing team (from Pardot) and the Customer Success team (from SFMC) to a single, new Adobe Marketo instance. This is the most transformative approach, designed to align the entire customer lifecycle—from the first touch to renewal and advocacy—on a single, B2B-centric platform.

Pros

  • Fully Unified Platform: Creates a single source of truth for all customer engagement.
  • 360-Degree Customer View: Enables a seamless view of the entire customer lifecycle for coordinated journeys.
  • Strong B2B Focus: Aligns the entire organization's outreach around Marketo's powerful account-based framework.
  • Reduced Vendor Complexity & Costs: Consolidates operations onto a single platform, reducing overhead long-term.

Cons

  • Highest Disruption: Requires migrating two teams from two different platforms simultaneously.
  • Sub-Optimal for CS: Marketo is not designed for customer service communications and lacks purpose-built SFMC features.
  • Likely More Change Management Needs: Requires comprehensive retraining for the Customer Success team.

Other potential options to consider

While these two are likely the ultimate options here are two additional consideration we could scope out and support:

Best-of-Breed Approach: Splitting CS and Marketing into two separate instances—one in SFMC and one in Marketo—to leverage the strengths of each platform for its intended purpose.

Orchestration Layer: Use Adobe Journey Optimizer (AJO) B2B as the orchestration layer above SFMC or Marketo. This allows for a 6sense-like, ABM-driven buying committee structure over all marketing efforts, regardless of the underlying platform.

Success stories

Proven results with industry leaders

Approach: SFMC Consolidation

Resmed: Unifying multiple platforms

Resmed operated two instances of Pardot (one of Gainsight, as well as a complex Marketo instance). We collaborated with them to merge these into a single SFMC instance linked to a third-party patient database. This consolidation enabled all marketing technology teams to operate under one central system. While they did not implement ABM, they effectively utilized a B2B2C approach in one of the business units, showcasing SFMC's flexibility.

Approach: Total Unification in Marketo

Contentsquare: Integrating acquisitions

After acquiring Heap and Hotjar, Contentsquare faced the challenge of integrating three separate marketing stacks (SFMC, HubSpot, Marketo). We assisted in unifying them into a new centralized Marketo instance with direct 6sense integration for ABM, and an ETL solution to Snowflake. This complex project resulted in significant cost savings and efficiency improvements across their global teams.

Approach: Best-of-Breed Strategy -- SFMC for CS & Marketo for Marketing

Atlassian: Platform consolidation at scale

Atlassian, through acquisition, had nearly 16 different Marketing Automation Platforms. We helped consolidate these into two distinct instances: Marketo for enterprise-level B2B ABM, and SFMC for engaging active users with nurturing and education. This decision was driven by the need for SFMC's strong in-app and push channel capabilities, which Marketo lacks. This strategic split allowed each platform to be used for its core strengths while ensuring deep integration with their CRM and data lake to support PLG strategies.
